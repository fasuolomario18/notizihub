## Hulu's Original Drama Is Actually Worth Your Time

"Second Chances" represents something increasingly rare in streaming: a show with genuine stakes and character development. Created by the showrunner behind the critically acclaimed series that ran for five seasons, this new drama follows six characters rebuilding their lives after major setbacks. The first season contains 10 episodes, and Hulu has already greenlit season two, which signals confidence rarely seen before official launch.

What makes this different from typical redemption narratives is the economic realism woven throughout. These characters don't land dream jobs or meet their soulmates in episode three. Instead, the writing tracks actual recovery timelines—therapy sessions, failed attempts, slow progress. It's the anti-Marvel approach to storytelling, which explains why it's been positioned by critics as "prestige television" rather than typical streaming content.

If you appreciated the "Mission Impossible" franchise but felt frustrated by its reliance on Tom Cruise's star power, this delivers similar technical ambition with ensemble casting. The film runs 142 minutes, but crucially, it doesn't waste runtime on exposition. The first action sequence occurs at the 8-minute mark.
