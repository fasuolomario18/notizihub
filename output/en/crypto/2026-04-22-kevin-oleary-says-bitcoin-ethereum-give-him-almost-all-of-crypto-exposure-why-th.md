## Bitcoin and Ethereum: The Foundation of Digital Assets

Bitcoin's position as the first and most recognized cryptocurrency remains unassailed in 2026. Despite the emergence of thousands of alternative cryptocurrencies, Bitcoin continues to capture the majority of institutional investment flows. Its fixed supply of 21 million coins, immutable blockchain ledger, and proof-of-work security model have made it synonymous with "digital gold" among traditional investors transitioning into crypto assets.

Ethereum, conversely, represents the evolution of blockchain technology beyond simple value transfer. Since its 2015 launch, Ethereum has established itself as the infrastructure layer for decentralized finance, non-fungible tokens, layer-2 scaling solutions, and countless other applications. The network's transition to proof-of-stake consensus in 2022 addressed previous environmental concerns and improved transaction efficiency, further validating its institutional legitimacy.

The dominance of Bitcoin and Ethereum in O'Leary's portfolio reflects their combined market capitalization and real-world usage. Together, these two cryptocurrencies account for the vast majority of blockchain activity, developer attention, and institutional investment. For an investor seeking exposure to cryptocurrency innovation without excessive concentration risk, Bitcoin and Ethereum form a logical foundation.

Moreover, both networks benefit from network effects—the phenomenon where a service becomes more valuable as more people use it. Bitcoin's security improves with each additional miner, while Ethereum's utility increases with each new developer building applications on its platform. These network effects create significant competitive moats that newer projects struggle to overcome, regardless of technological improvements they might offer.
