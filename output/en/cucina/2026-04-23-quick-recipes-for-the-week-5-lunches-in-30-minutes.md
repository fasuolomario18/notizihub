## One-Pan Mediterranean Shrimp Pasta

This vibrant Mediterranean-inspired dish combines protein, vegetables, and whole grains in a single pan, making cleanup as quick as the cooking process itself.

**Ingredients:**
- 12 oz whole wheat pasta
- 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup fresh spinach
- ¼ cup Kalamata olives
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes to taste
- Fresh basil for garnish

**Instructions:**

Begin by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil and cooking your pasta according to package directions. While the water heats, warm olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant—this builds flavor without requiring extra time.

Add your shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side until they turn pink and opaque. This quick protein cooking is one of the advantages of choosing shrimp for rapid recipes. Once the shrimp is cooked, remove it from the pan and set aside.

In the same skillet, add your halved cherry tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften and release their juices. The natural acidity creates a light sauce without requiring cream or complicated preparation. Toss in the fresh spinach and olives, stirring until the spinach wilts.

Drain your cooked pasta and add it to the skillet along with the reserved shrimp. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over everything and toss to combine. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of red pepper flakes for warmth. Finish with fresh basil and serve immediately.

**Time-Saving Tip:** Purchase pre-peeled and deveined shrimp to cut prep time significantly.

## Thai Green Curry Chicken Bowl

This aromatic, flavorful curry proves that quick cooking and exotic flavors aren't mutually exclusive. The combination of spicy, sweet, and savory elements creates a deeply satisfying lunch.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 3 tablespoons Thai green curry paste
- 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
- 2 cups jasmine rice (cooked in advance)
- 1 bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup green beans
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon brown sugar
- Fresh Thai basil and lime wedges

**Instructions:**

If you haven't prepared rice in advance, start it first—jasmine rice typically cooks in about 15 minutes. For truly quick cooking, consider using minute rice or pre-cooked rice packets available at most grocery stores.

Heat a large pot or wok over medium-high heat. Add curry paste and cook for about 1 minute, stirring constantly. The brief cooking time releases the aromatic oils while maintaining the curry's fresh flavor. Pour in your coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.

Add your diced chicken pieces to the simmering curry. The chicken cooks quickly in the hot liquid—usually 8-10 minutes until fully cooked through. While the chicken cooks, prepare your vegetables by slicing the bell pepper and trimming the green beans.

Add the prepared vegetables to the curry along with minced garlic. Continue simmering for 5 minutes until the vegetables achieve tender-crisp texture. Season with fish sauce and brown sugar, which balance the spice with sweetness and umami depth.

Serve the fragrant curry over your prepared jasmine rice, garnishing with fresh Thai basil and lime wedges. The brightness of fresh lime juice completes this quick cooking masterpiece.

## Quick Cooking Ideas for Busy Weekdays

### Turkey and Avocado Wraps with Herb Crema
- Spread herb crema (sour cream mixed with cilantro, lime, and garlic) on whole wheat tortillas
- Layer with sliced roasted turkey, fresh avocado, crisp lettuce, and diced tomatoes
- Roll tightly and wrap in foil for easy transport
- Total time: 8 minutes
- Perfect for meal prep—make several at once

### Caprese Chicken Salad with Pesto Dressing
- Grill or pan-sear chicken breasts until cooked through (about 10 minutes)
- Slice and arrange over mixed greens with fresh mozzarella and heirloom tomatoes
- Drizzle with balsamic-pesto vinaigrette
- Top with fresh basil and croutons
- Total time: 15 minutes
- Celebrates fresh ingredients with minimal cooking

### Asian Beef Stir-Fry with Quick Vegetables
- Slice beef tenderloin thinly for rapid cooking
- Stir-fry in a hot wok with pre-cut vegetables: broccoli, snap peas, carrots, and mushrooms
- Create a sauce with soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and sesame oil
- Serve over brown rice or noodles
- Total time: 20 minutes including sauce preparation
- High in protein and vegetables for sustained energy

### Salmon with Lemon-Herb Butter and Roasted Vegetables
- Season salmon fillets with salt, pepper, and herbs
- Bake at 400°F for 12-15 minutes while preparing vegetables
- Roast cherry tomatoes, zucchini, and Brussels sprouts simultaneously
- Top salmon with lemon-herb butter for elegance without complexity
- Total time: 25 minutes
- Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidants

### Chickpea Buddha Bowl with Tahini Dressing
- Combine cooked chickpeas with roasted red peppers, cucumber, and red onion
- Add cooked quinoa or farro for sustaining grains
- Dress with tahini-based sauce (tahini, lemon juice, garlic, water)
- Garnish with pomegranate seeds and fresh herbs
- Total time: 10 minutes (if using canned chickpeas and pre-cooked grains)
- Completely vegetarian and packed with fiber

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How can I prepare these quick recipes for meal prep?**
A: Most of these recipes can be prepared in batches and stored in glass containers for 3-4 days. Cook proteins and grains in advance, then assemble fresh components like vegetables and dressings just before eating to maintain texture and flavor quality.

**Q: What pantry staples should I keep for quick cooking?**
A: Essential quick-cooking ingredients include curry paste, coconut milk, good quality olive oil, fresh garlic, various spices, canned chickpeas, whole grain pasta, and rice. Having these basics on hand means you can prepare satisfying meals even with limited fresh ingredients.

**Q: Can I substitute ingredients in these recipes?**
A: Absolutely. These recipes are frameworks, not rigid instructions. Substitute any protein with your preference, swap vegetables based on seasonal availability, and adjust spices to your taste preferences. The cooking techniques remain the same regardless of specific ingredients.

**Q: How do I ensure proteins cook quickly without becoming dry?**
A: Choose quick-cooking proteins like shrimp, thin chicken breasts, or fish fillets. Don't overcrowd the pan, allowing proper heat circulation. Use a meat thermometer to check doneness without guessing. Remove proteins from heat slightly before fully cooked—carryover cooking continues the process.

**Q: What equipment helps with quick cooking at home?**
A: A sharp chef's knife reduces prep time significantly. A good non-stick skillet, large pot, and wok enable rapid cooking. A rice cooker or instant pot eliminates waiting time for grains. Having quality tools isn't essential but dramatically improves efficiency for quick cooking.
