## Component Selection Strategy for €800 PC Gaming

When building a PC on a strict €800 budget, strategic component selection is crucial. You must balance raw performance with long-term value. The key is identifying which components offer the best performance-per-euro ratio while avoiding unnecessary expenses.

Start by allocating your budget roughly as follows: CPU (€200-250), GPU (€300-350), motherboard (€80-100), RAM (€60-80), storage (€70-100), and power supply plus case (€80-100). This distribution ensures your GPU receives appropriate investment—the most critical component for gaming performance—while maintaining a balanced system that won't create bottlenecks.

You'll find components that would cost €400+ new available for €250-300, dramatically expanding your gaming capabilities within the €800 constraint.

## The Ultimate €800 PC Gaming Build Components

**Processor:** A mid-range Ryzen 5 or Intel Core i5 from recent generations provides excellent gaming performance without demanding premium pricing. These CPUs handle modern AAA titles smoothly while remaining budget-friendly at €200-240.

**Graphics Card:** This is your budget's anchor. Look for used RTX 3060 Ti or RTX 4060 Super models, which deliver 1440p gaming at high settings. A new RTX 4060 (€270-300) represents excellent value for 1440p gaming, outperforming PS5 in pure horsepower while costing less than console equivalents.

**Motherboard:** B750 or B650 boards offer excellent features without enthusiast pricing. €80-100 secures a reliable platform supporting future CPU upgrades.

**Memory:** 16GB DDR5 RAM is standard in 2026, available for €60-70. This matches console performance while offering system-wide benefits beyond gaming.

**Storage:** A 1TB NVMe SSD (€50-70) provides adequate space for a gaming library. PS5 and Xbox Series X feature similar storage, but PC's upgrade path is far more flexible.

**Power Supply:** A quality 650-750W semi-modular unit from reputable brands costs €70-90, ensuring stable power delivery and longevity.

**Case:** Budget cases like NZXT H510 Flow or Corsair 4000D Airflow cost €50-70 and offer solid cooling with clean aesthetics.

## Why PC Gaming Under €800 Beats Consoles

### Performance Advantages
A €800 PC gaming build delivers better raw performance than PS5 or Xbox Series X in many scenarios. While consoles optimize software for hardware, PCs offer superior frame rates and resolution options through driver updates and optimization patches.

### Game Library and Cost Savings
Steam provides access to hundreds of thousands of games, including free-to-play titles, which consoles cannot match. Steam sales regularly discount AAA titles 50-70%, meaning your game spending decreases dramatically compared to console purchases at €60-70 per title.

## Building Your €800 PC: Essential Tips

- **Buy during sales:** Monitor pricing 2-3 months before building. Black Friday or summer sales can reduce component costs 10-20%.
- **Consider refurbished components:** Certified refurbished GPUs from retailers offer warranties at reduced prices.
- **Explore the used market carefully:** Purchase from reputable sellers with return policies. Verify hardware functionality before finalizing purchases.
- **Avoid bottlenecks:** Balance CPU and GPU investments to prevent one component from severely limiting the other.
- **Plan for peripherals separately:** Budget doesn't include monitor, keyboard, or mouse. Reusing peripherals from older systems helps stretch your budget.
- **Prioritize power supply quality:** Never compromise here; a failed PSU can destroy other components.

## Building Skills and Future Upgrading

Building your PC takes 1-2 hours for beginners and requires only a screwdriver and anti-static precautions. Countless YouTube guides walk through assembly step-by-step. This hands-on experience teaches valuable tech skills while saving €100-200 in assembly labor.

Once built, upgrading becomes straightforward. Next year, swapping the GPU for a newer model costs minutes and enables gaming at higher settings without rebuilding from scratch—something console owners cannot do.
